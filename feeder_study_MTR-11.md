# Feeder engineering study - MTR-11

## 1. Scope of this document

This engineering study is the planning office's working record for distribution
feeder MTR-11. It is a declared-synthetic scenario document prepared for the
fast-track adjudication exercise. All figures below are scenario inputs derived
from the public screen structures; they are not real measurements. Where a
fast-track screen needs a feeder value, take it from the specific labelled figure
in this study; do not read a value off a summary table, because there is no
summary table, and do not assume the largest number on the page is the governing
one.

## 2. Substation and circuit description

Feeder MTR-11 is a radial distribution circuit served from its substation bank
through automatic sectionalizing devices. The circuit carries a mix of
residential and light-commercial load with several existing distributed energy
resource (DER) interconnections. Protection coordination is maintained by the
substation relay and downstream reclosers; the line section bounded by the
automatic sectionalizing devices is the unit against which the 15 percent screen
is evaluated for this feeder.

## 4. Existing generation on the circuit

Distributed generation on MTR-11 falls into three buckets. Only generation that is
ACTUALLY IN SERVICE counts as existing generation for the 15 percent aggregate
screen. Reserved and under-study capacity is not yet energised and must not be
added to the existing-generation baseline.

- In-service distributed generation (counts toward the screen): 0.55 MW.
- Reserved capacity (executed agreements, not yet in service): 0.59 MW.
- Under-study capacity (queued, screening not complete): 0.39 MW.
- Total interconnected-or-pending nameplate on file: 1.53 MW.

The existing-generation baseline for the screen on MTR-11 is the in-service figure,
0.55 MW. Do not use the total-on-file figure (1.53 MW)
or any sum that includes reserved or under-study capacity as the baseline.

## 5. Hosting capacity and short-circuit

- Published hosting capacity for MTR-11: 1.4 MW.
- Short-circuit interrupting-capability utilisation at the substation device:
  61.0 percent. A proposed interconnection may not push any protective device
  above 87.5 percent of its short-circuit interrupting capability.

## 6. Screen reference for this feeder

- Fifteen percent of the most recently measured (2024) peak = 0.15 x 7.2 MW.
- Existing in-service generation to start any aggregate running total: 0.55 MW.
- These two figures, read from sections 3 and 4 above, are what the fast-track
  15 percent screen for MTR-11 is evaluated against.
