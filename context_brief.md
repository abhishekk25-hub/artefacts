# Planning office context brief

After recent public scrutiny of distribution outages and fire risk, the interconnection planning office is hardening its fast-track screening. The real regulatory backdrop for these screens is public: FERC SGIP (https://www.ferc.gov/sites/default/files/2020-04/sm-gen-procedures.pdf), California Rule 21 (https://www.pge.com/tariffs/assets/pdf/tariffbook/ELEC_RULES_21.pdf), and the New York State SIR used by Con Edison (https://dps.ny.gov/system/files/documents/2026/05/24-e-0621-sirs-effective-february-9-2026.pdf). This brief and the queue are the office's own working artifacts; the tariff excerpts are verbatim from those public sources and the feeder grid figures are the real per-circuit values published in PG&E's Grid Resource Integration Portal / Distribution Resource Planning and ICA data (https://www.pge.com/en/about/doing-business-with-pge/interconnections/distributed-resource-planning-data-and-maps.html), retrieved 2026-07-03 from the public ArcGIS service (https://services2.arcgis.com/mJaJSax0KPHoCNB6/arcgis/rest/services/DRPComplianceRelProd/FeatureServer).

Feeder grid data is published as one engineering study per feeder (`feeder_study_<feeder_id>.md`), not as a summary table. Each study carries the feeder's measured peak history and 2029 planning forecast, in-service versus reserved/under-study generation, ICA hosting capacity, and facility-loading utilisation. The screen basis for a feeder is the most recently measured 2025 base-year peak and the in-service existing generation; the 2029 forecast peak and reserved/under-study generation in the studies are context, not the screen basis. Fetch the study for each feeder that appears in the queue and read the governing figures out of it.
