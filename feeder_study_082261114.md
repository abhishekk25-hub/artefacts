# Feeder engineering study - 082261114 (SAN JOSE B 1114)

## 1. Scope of this document

This engineering study is the planning office's working record for PG&E
distribution feeder 082261114 (SAN JOSE B 1114), served from the SAN JOSE B substation on the
San Jose division at 12 kV. The figures below are drawn from PG&E's public Grid
Resource Integration Portal / Distribution Resource Planning (DRP) and
Integration Capacity Analysis (ICA) publication for this circuit. Where a
fast-track screen needs a feeder value, take it from the specific labelled figure
in this study; do not read a value off a summary table, because there is no
summary table, and do not assume the largest number on the page is the governing
one.

## 2. Substation and circuit description

Feeder 082261114 is a radial distribution circuit served from the SAN JOSE B bank through
automatic sectionalizing devices. The circuit carries a mix of residential and
light-commercial load with existing distributed energy resource (DER)
interconnections. Protection coordination is maintained by the substation relay
and downstream reclosers; the line section bounded by the automatic
sectionalizing devices is the unit against which the 15 percent screen is
evaluated for this feeder. The published facility rating is 12.83 MW.

## 4. Existing generation on the circuit

Distributed generation on 082261114 falls into three buckets. Only generation that is
ACTUALLY IN SERVICE counts as existing generation for the 15 percent aggregate
screen. Reserved and under-study capacity is not yet energised and must not be
added to the existing-generation baseline.

- In-service distributed generation (counts toward the screen): 0.081 MW.
- Reserved capacity (executed agreements, not yet in service): 0.111 MW.
- Under-study capacity (queued, screening not complete): 0.074 MW.
- Total interconnected-or-pending nameplate on file: 0.266 MW.

The existing-generation baseline for the screen on 082261114 is the in-service figure,
0.081 MW. Do not use the total-on-file figure (0.266 MW) or any sum that
includes reserved or under-study capacity as the baseline.

## 5. Hosting capacity and facility loading

- Published ICA generation hosting capacity for 082261114: 1.75 MW.
- Facility loading utilisation against the 12.83 MW rating: 71.2 percent. A
  proposed interconnection may not push any protective device above 87.5 percent
  of its short-circuit interrupting capability.

## 6. Screen reference for this feeder

- Fifteen percent of the most recently measured 2025 base-year peak =
  0.15 x 9.13 MW.
- Existing in-service generation to start any aggregate running total: 0.081 MW.
- These two figures, read from sections 3 and 4 above, are what the fast-track
  15 percent screen for 082261114 is evaluated against.
