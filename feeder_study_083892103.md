# Feeder engineering study - 083892103 (MONTAGUE 2103)

## 1. Scope of this document

This engineering study is the planning office's working record for PG&E
distribution feeder 083892103 (MONTAGUE 2103), served from the MONTAGUE substation on the
San Jose division at 21 kV. The figures below are drawn from PG&E's public Grid
Resource Integration Portal / Distribution Resource Planning (DRP) and
Integration Capacity Analysis (ICA) publication for this circuit. Where a
fast-track screen needs a feeder value, take it from the specific labelled figure
in this study; do not read a value off a summary table, because there is no
summary table, and do not assume the largest number on the page is the governing
one.

## 2. Substation and circuit description

Feeder 083892103 is a radial distribution circuit served from the MONTAGUE bank through
automatic sectionalizing devices. The circuit carries a mix of residential and
light-commercial load with existing distributed energy resource (DER)
interconnections. Protection coordination is maintained by the substation relay
and downstream reclosers; the line section bounded by the automatic
sectionalizing devices is the unit against which the 15 percent screen is
evaluated for this feeder. The published facility rating is 20.04 MW.

The table below is the annual facility peak loading as published in the DRP data.
Note that the utility screens are evaluated against the MOST RECENTLY MEASURED
annual peak - the 2025 base-year loading. Older measured vintages and the forward
planning forecast appear here for planning context only and are NOT the screen
basis.

| vintage | basis | facility loading (MW) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2021 | historical measured | 16.01 |
| 2023 | historical measured | 17.08 |
| 2025 | most recently measured (base year) | 17.79 |

For the fast-track screens on 083892103 the governing figure is the most recently

superseded.

## 4. Existing generation on the circuit

Distributed generation on 083892103 falls into three buckets. Only generation that is
ACTUALLY IN SERVICE counts as existing generation for the 15 percent aggregate
screen. Reserved and under-study capacity is not yet energised and must not be
added to the existing-generation baseline.

- In-service distributed generation (counts toward the screen): 0.44 MW.
- Reserved capacity (executed agreements, not yet in service): 0.264 MW.
- Under-study capacity (queued, screening not complete): 0.176 MW.
- Total interconnected-or-pending nameplate on file: 0.88 MW.

The existing-generation baseline for the screen on 083892103 is the in-service figure,
0.44 MW. Do not use the total-on-file figure (0.88 MW) or any sum that
includes reserved or under-study capacity as the baseline.

## 5. Hosting capacity and facility loading

- Published ICA generation hosting capacity for 083892103: 1.543 MW.
- Facility loading utilisation against the 20.04 MW rating: 86.5 percent. A
  proposed interconnection may not push any protective device above 87.5 percent
  of its short-circuit interrupting capability.

## 6. Screen reference for this feeder

- Fifteen percent of the most recently measured 2025 base-year peak =
  0.15 x 17.79 MW.
- Existing in-service generation to start any aggregate running total: 0.44 MW.
- These two figures, read from sections 3 and 4 above, are what the fast-track
  15 percent screen for 083892103 is evaluated against.
